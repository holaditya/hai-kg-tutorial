Element Categories

When analyzing StarCraft II screenshots, we categorize game elements based on their strategic importance and urgency:

Good

Routine/Positive situations that support your strategy:

  • Workers mining resources normally
  • Completed defensive structures
  • Successful tech upgrades
  • Stable economy
Warning

Situations requiring attention but not immediate action:

  • Enemy scouts in your territory
  • Low on a particular resource
  • Defensive gaps
  • Enemy building military units
Critical

Urgent situations requiring immediate action:

  • Direct attacks on your base
  • Running out of essential resources
  • Key structures under threat
  • Major strategic disadvantages

Relationship Types

Relationships describe how game elements interact with each other:

The Five Relationship Categories:

Is Actively Supporting/Enabling

One element is currently helping or powering another (e.g., Supply Depot → Army, Pylon → Protoss Buildings)

Can Enable

One element has the potential to help another but isn't actively doing so (e.g., Idle Worker → Resource Gathering)

Blocks

One element is preventing another from functioning (e.g., Enemy Unit → Your Expansion, Supply Block → Unit Production)

Threatens

One element poses a danger to another (e.g., Enemy Army → Your Base, Siege Tank → Enemy Units)

Currently Has No Impact

Elements are present but not significantly affecting each other at the moment
